# Good Story Studios — Creator Profiles for Sponsorship Matching

Purpose: Used by the monthly film slate automation to match upcoming films to the right creator for sponsored segment pitches. Focused on content format, niche, brand fit, and audience — not biography.

---

## Airrack (Eric Decker)
**Channel:** Airrack | ~18.5M subscribers | YouTube
**Niche:** Large-scale stunts, Guinness World Record attempts, social experiments, high-energy challenges
**Signature formats:** "30 Days..." series (commits to a challenge for 30 consecutive days), "I Survived..." format (extreme environment challenges), brand-integrated event stunts where the product IS the challenge (e.g. Pizza Hut Guinness Record pizza)
**Past sponsors:** Pizza Hut, Prime, G Fuel, Chipotle
**Audience:** 16–28, male-skewing, mainstream YouTube, high energy
**Best film matches:** Action, heist, sports, challenge/competition, food, any film with a record-breaking or extreme premise
**Integration style:** Brand becomes the centerpiece of a stunt or challenge — not a read, an event. The product is woven into the video concept itself.

---

## Yes Theory (Thomas Brag, Ammar Kandil, Matt Dajer)
**Channel:** Yes Theory | ~9.5M subscribers | YouTube
**Niche:** Adventure, travel, social experiments built around saying yes to discomfort; "Seek Discomfort" philosophy
**Signature formats:** Saying yes to strangers' dares, extreme travel challenges, documentary-style human connection experiments, collaborative stunts with public figures
**Past sponsors:** Travel, lifestyle, and outdoor brands
**Audience:** 18–32, globally-minded, adventurous, aspirational
**Best film matches:** Adventure, survival, travel, self-discovery, human connection, overcoming fear, underdog stories, international settings
**Integration style:** The brand should connect to courage, curiosity, or shared humanity. Best when the sponsored segment itself involves a "seek discomfort" challenge or experience tied to the film's themes.

## Drew Binsky (Drew Goldberg)
**Channel:** Drew Binsky | ~5.5M subscribers | YouTube + 18M cross-platform
**Niche:** Travel to all 197 countries, cultural deep dives, off-the-beaten-path destinations, geopolitics through travel
**Signature formats:** Country-specific documentary shorts ("X Things About [Country]"), cultural explainers, "I visited every country" milestone content
**Past sponsors:** Travel brands, fintech, tech (VPN, booking apps, financial services)
**Audience:** 25–42, travel enthusiasts, globally curious, educated
**Best film matches:** Films set in international or exotic locations, cultural stories, political thrillers, diaspora narratives, war/conflict films with a human angle
**Integration style:** Drew visits a location tied to the film's setting or theme and creates a mini-documentary. The brand fits naturally as a travel tool or cultural connection.

## Alberta Tech (Alberta Devor)
**Channel:** Alberta Tech | ~750K+ cross-platform
**Niche:** AI and tech industry commentary through a comedy lens; software engineer perspective on Silicon Valley culture
**Signature formats:** Comedic explainers on AI news and tech trends, satirical takes on startup culture, reaction/commentary on tech industry events
**Past sponsors:** SaaS, tech tools, developer-adjacent brands
**Audience:** 20–35, software engineers, AI enthusiasts, tech workers, CS students
**Best film matches:** Sci-fi, AI/technology thrillers, hacker/programmer narratives, films about Silicon Valley or startup culture, dystopian tech futures
**Integration style:** Alberta ties the film's tech premise to a real current AI/tech trend and breaks it down with humor. Works best when the film has a concept her audience would find either impressive or absurd.

## Theorist Media (Game Theory, Film Theory, Food Theory, Style Theory)
**Channels:** Game Theory (~18M), Film Theory (~14M), Food Theory (~10M), Style Theory (~2M) | ~44M combined
**Niche:** Deep analytical dives into games, films, food, and fashion — finding hidden lore, real-world science, and unexpected theories
**Signature formats:** "Film Theory:" — analyzes upcoming or existing films for plot holes, hidden science, or lore deep dives; "Game Theory:" — game narrative and mechanics analysis; "Food Theory:" — food science and industry explainers
**Past sponsors:** Gaming, streaming, and food brands
**Audience:** 13–26, intellectually curious, pop culture obsessed
**Best film matches:** Any major film release — Film Theory specializes in pre-release hype content, sequels with lore, sci-fi with real science angles, animated films, franchise films
**Integration style:** Film Theory builds a "theory" video around the film's premise or science — perfect pre-release hype that drives ticket sales. The sponsor is woven into the theory reveal.

## Marko Terzo
**Channel:** Marko Terzo | ~8M subscribers | YouTube
**Niche:** Sneaker and shoe customization, wearable art, high-profile brand collaborations with athletes and celebrities
**Signature formats:** Custom sneaker build reveals (often for NBA/NFL athletes or celebrities), brand collaboration process videos, speed-art customs, "I customized X for Y" format
**Past sponsors:** Nike, Converse, Adidas, Adobe
**Audience:** 16–32, sneakerheads, streetwear and fashion enthusiasts, art and design fans
**Best film matches:** Films with strong fashion or street culture angles, sports films, films featuring athletes or musicians, art/creativity themes, superhero or franchise films (custom themed sneakers)
**Integration style:** Marko creates a one-of-a-kind custom sneaker or wearable piece inspired by the film's aesthetic or characters, then reveals it on camera. The film studio becomes the "client" for a custom build — incredibly shareable content.

## Joon Lee (Morning Announcements)
**Channel:** Morning Announcements | Growing — ex-ESPN journalist
**Niche:** Long-form sports journalism and storytelling; narrative-driven sports reporting for YouTube audiences
**Signature formats:** In-depth athlete profiles, investigative sports stories, behind-the-scenes access pieces, underdog narratives
**Past sponsors:** Sports, tech, and finance brands aligned with a serious journalism audience
**Audience:** 22–42, sports fans who want depth over highlights, former ESPN demographic
**Best film matches:** Sports films, athlete biopics, underdog stories, competition and rivalry narratives, any film based on a true sports story
**Integration style:** Joon builds a companion journalism piece — a mini-documentary or reported segment that explores the real-world story behind the film's premise, with the brand as the sponsor of the investigative segment.

## Jesse Michels (American Alchemy)
**Channel:** American Alchemy | ~500K+ cross-platform
**Niche:** Frontier science, UAP/UFO research, longevity, quantum physics, consciousness, and fringe technology; backed by Thiel Capital
**Signature formats:** Long-form interviews with scientists, researchers, and government insiders; documentary explorations of topics mainstream media ignores; deep dives into fringe but credible science
**Past sponsors:** Science, deep-tech, and defense-adjacent brands
**Audience:** Scientists, Pentagon and intelligence community members, tech billionaires, frontier researchers, deeply curious intellectuals — 28–50, extremely high-value niche
**Best film matches:** Sci-fi, space exploration, consciousness or AI films, conspiracy/mystery thrillers, films exploring the unknown, UAP or paranormal themes, government secrets narratives
**Integration style:** Jesse connects the film's premise to real frontier science or classified research — making the film feel like it's based on things actually happening. The sponsored segment positions Jesse (and the brand) at the intersection of entertainment and cutting-edge reality.

## Grant Rudow
**Channel:** Grant Rudow | ~129K subscribers | YouTube (growing)
**Niche:** Business, finance, and economics — "Big Ideas. Simply Explained."
**Signature formats:** Clean, minimal explainer videos on macroeconomic concepts, company origin stories, financial crises, business model breakdowns
**Past sponsors:** Fintech, SaaS, business tools, financial services
**Audience:** 22–40, finance professionals, business students, entrepreneurs, MBA-types
**Best film matches:** Business dramas, Wall Street films, financial thrillers, entrepreneur biopics, economic crisis narratives, heist films with financial mechanics, corporate power stories
**Integration style:** Grant explains the real financial or economic concept that the film dramatizes — positioning the film as the exciting version of something he covers analytically. The brand sponsors the "real story behind the movie" explainer segment.
